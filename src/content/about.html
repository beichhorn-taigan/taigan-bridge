About Taigan Bridge 対岸

Taigan (対岸) is a Japanese word meaning "the opposite shore" — the bank on the far side of a river, or the coast across an ocean.

There is a famous Japanese expression — 対岸の火事 (taigan no kaji), "a fire on the opposite shore" — used when someone watches a problem from a safe distance, treating it as someone else's concern.

For Americans living in Japan, the opposite shore is not someone else's problem. It is home. And the financial, tax, estate, and health questions that follow you across the Pacific cannot be watched from a distance — they have to be organized, in two countries, in two languages, across two legal systems, two medical systems, and two sets of expectations about what "in order" means.

Taigan Bridge is the bridge between those two shores. It is a tool for keeping your life on both sides of the Pacific in order — whether you arrived under SOFA orders, on a work or spouse visa, or were born here as a dual citizen.

Who this is for

Any U.S. person — citizen, green-card holder, or accidental American by birth — whose life touches both the United States and Japan. The tool adapts to your specific situation through onboarding:

  • Long-term Japan residents on work, spouse, or Long-Term Resident visas — most of the audience. Annual 確定申告 prep, the 10-year worldwide-asset clock for inheritance tax, NHI premium timing, Furusato Nozei limits, mortgage credit, and the U.S. tax obligations that follow you regardless of how long you've been here.
  • SOFA-status individuals — active duty, DoD civilians, and SOFA contractors. The window between SOFA status and 住民票 registration is where extraordinary tax-planning mistakes are made; the SOFA Roth Sequencing Planner exists to surface that window.
  • U.S. veterans in Japan — VA benefits, FMP for service-connected care abroad, TRICARE Overseas, GI Bill timing, and survivor benefits, all status-aware (active / reserve / retired / separated).
  • Mixed-citizenship families — Japanese-national spouses, dual-citizen children with their 国籍選択 deadlines, per-child FBAR considerations, education savings (529 vs 教育資金一括贈与), and family inheritance pre-positioning.
  • Future expats planning a move TO Japan — surface the things you should do BEFORE 住民票 registration changes the tax math.

What this is

An organizational tool. A scenario modeler. A place to keep the moving pieces of a cross-border life in one structured place that you control. Today the tool covers:

  • Net worth + accounts — multi-currency asset tracking with year-end snapshots, FBAR threshold monitoring, beneficiary review, and PFIC scanning. AI-assisted statement extraction from images / PDFs.
  • FBAR (FinCEN 114) — full year-by-year filing tracker with Treasury year-end rates, multi-filer households, and image-import for passbooks + bank statements.
  • Tax coordinator — bilingual deadline calendar, forms-applicability assessment (1040, 2555, 1116, 8938, 8621, 確定申告), document checklist with Document Vault sync, and a JP-side filing-responsibility split for SOFA + JP-spouse households.
  • Projections + decumulation — long-horizon modeling with NIIT, IRMAA, state tax, Roth conversion ladder, Social Security claiming strategy, RMD timing, and JP pension vesting paths (国民年金 / 厚生年金 / 追納 / 任意加入 / 合算 / カラ期間).
  • Estate planning — JP statutory shares (民法 §887–§890), inheritance tax estimation, will tracker, 戸籍 handling for foreign decedents, and a Letter of Instruction generator with the survivor guide HTML below.
  • Property — JP + US real estate with 固定資産税 tracking, §121 / §469 / §1250 cross-border treatment, and 古民家 / 農地 inheritance scenarios. AI extraction from 固定資産税通知書.
  • FX + cross-border banking — live mid-market USD/JPY reference (informational), Treasury rate (used for FBAR), and a transfer-cost calculator across major remittance platforms (Wise, Revolut, SBI Sumishin, Sony Bank, Schwab International, etc.).
  • Health tracker — exam history, lab results with bilingual reference ranges and AI-generated explanations, dental periodontal tracking with per-tooth findings, medication list, insurance card capture, invoice import, care episodes, and a printable Year-in-Review.
  • Healthcare module — Medicare Part B / IRMAA tracking, FEHB / TRICARE / FMP coverage routing, Japan NHI / SHI premium estimation, and long-term-care (介護保険) timing.
  • Family + dual citizenship — 国籍選択 (Japanese Nationality Law Article 14) deadline tracking, passport renewal with vision-import from photos / PDFs (令和 → ISO conversion handled), education savings, and gift pre-positioning vehicles.
  • Document Vault — inventory of where everything lives (passport, will, deed, insurance, tax returns), with AI-assisted document classification and expiry alerts.
  • Contacts — unified address book that auto-pulls from every other module (medical providers, insurance plans, family, professionals, built-in emergency numbers for Japan).
  • Action Center — the "what should I do today?" surface that aggregates time-sensitive items from every module: FBAR deadlines, document expiries, RMD windows, passport renewals, backup overdue, and more.
  • Sharing + backup — Spouse Handoff HTML (bilingual when a JP spouse is detected), Survivor Guide (formatted for printing and storing with the will), Advisor JSON, and one-click full-state backup with optional auto-prompt schedule.
  • Ask Taigan — an opt-in AI assistant that has read-only access to your Taigan Bridge state and answers situation-specific questions in either language. Uses your Claude API key.

Everything runs in your browser. Your data lives in your browser's local storage, on your machine, and is never sent to the author or to any server. You can export a JSON backup at any time and you can delete everything with one click.

The optional AI-assisted features — document extraction from photos and PDFs, account-statement parsing, glossary lookups, Ask Taigan — use your own Claude API key, connecting directly from your browser to Anthropic. The author has no visibility into your conversations, your data, or your usage.

What this is not

Not financial advice. Not tax advice. Not legal advice. Not medical advice. Not an investment recommendation. Not a substitute for a CPA, tax attorney, estate attorney, licensed financial advisor, Japanese 税理士, or your physician.

Cross-border tax and estate planning is genuinely difficult. Japanese inheritance tax is among the world's most aggressive. PFIC rules make most Japanese investment vehicles toxic for U.S. persons. The 10-year clock changes the global-asset tax base. The Health Tracker organizes what your doctor told you — it does not interpret labs, recommend treatment, or substitute for clinical judgment. The most expensive mistakes — financial and medical — are the ones made without a qualified professional in the loop.

Use this tool to organize your thinking, surface the right questions, and prepare a complete picture to bring to qualified professionals — then act on their guidance, not on calculator output.

Who built this

Taigan Bridge was built by Benjamin Eichhorn — a U.S. defense contractor based in Tokyo, with twenty years of experience working on U.S.–Japan programs. Husband to a Japanese national, father to three dual-citizen children, and someone who has personally navigated 住民票 registration, 確定申告 filings, FBAR reporting, Japanese inheritance considerations, the JP medical system from the U.S.-insurance side, and the question of where to retire on the opposite shore.

This tool exists because no commercial financial planning product addresses the specific situation of Americans living in Japan — not the 10-year worldwide-asset clock, not the PFIC trap that poisons most local investment options, not the SOFA-status edge cases, not the bilingual healthcare-record problem, and not the practical "where is everything" organization that lets a non-Japanese-reading spouse or executor pick up the pieces in an emergency.
